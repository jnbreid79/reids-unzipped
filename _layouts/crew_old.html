The crew
Isla "First Mate"
Built-in chess table

Isla's best thing about Zipper is that she has a built-in chess board on the cockpit table. She would be an "Otter" if she was at school in Itchen Abbas this year — appropriately enough she has chosen to be an otter for boat-school.

"I've never lost at chess to Daddy"

— Isla Reid
Angus "Captain"
Hide-and-seek

Angus' best thing about Zipper is that she's great for hide-and-seek — there are so many places to hide. He completed his reception year at school before we set sail, and for boat-school has chosen to be a "sea turtle".

"My superpower is Lego"

— Angus Reid
Sophie "Ship's Cook"
I can see!

Sophie's favourite thing about Zipper is that she can see when she's helming — at 5'3" this is something she's not used to on other boats. Headmistress, chief medic and head chef, she's in charge of everything that keeps the team alive.

"Doctors spend 7 years training, I did it in 4 days"

— Sophie Reid, to Dr. Nigel Reid
Jamie "Cabin Boy"
2-in-1

Jamie's favourite thing about Zipper is that she is a catamaran; two-boats-in-one, the port hull for the children and starboard for us. The engineer keeping the mechanical and electrical systems running, he also leads the sailing programme.

"This will be easy, it's going to be downwind warm weather sailing"

— Jamie Reid (before leaving Northern France, reefed down into a headwind, wearing every item of clothing aboard)
Guest appearances

Friends, family & hitchhikers who’ve joined along the way

On our way round we are welcoming family and friends aboard at various times — both to come and say hi, and to help with the sailing, particularly on longer passages.

B
Bumpy Shakedown: Cherbourg → St Malo

Bumpy joined us for our very first passage on Zipper, anchoring off Sark overnight on the way. A natural sailor, and the inspiration behind the name of this blog.

T
Tom Bay of Biscay: Roscoff → Lanzarote

MVP for the Hill Climb game on his phone — Angus is still obsessed.

N
Neal Bay of Biscay: Roscoff → Lanzarote

Closely fought but second place with Wordscape. Another winner on the phone!

J
Dottie (Julia) Atlantic crossing: Tenerife → Mindelo → Barbados

Treasure hunt organiser, keeping the children on their toes every other day for prizes hidden around the boat

R
Grumpy (Rob) Atlantic crossing: Tenerife → Mindelo → Barbados

Daily sunset optimist ("it's definitely going to be colourful this time") and welcome icecream supplier
