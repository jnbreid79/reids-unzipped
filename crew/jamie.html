Jamie leads the sailing programme and on-water logistics — demonstrating he hasn't progressed that far since his school and university days as head of boats and social sec. The schedule is broad: organising which toys to use on which days is no mean feat with paddle boarding, kitesurfing, dinghy sailing, body surfing, beach cricket, snorkelling, diving and fishing amongst the available kit to choose from. Now, where can we find a cheap wingfoil setup from?

He also takes on most things engineering — keeping the boat's mechanical and electrical systems running, including offshore and onshore comms. This in itself is a full time job and, again, harks back to university - trying to drag long-buried engineering skills into the real world (or, YouTube!)

The jobs list is long and ever-growing; new items are added more quickly than the existing jobs are ticked off. From a grinding autopilot in the Atlantic to a broken gear cable in the Tuamotus, stalling engines in the Marquesas to a failing regulator in Tonga — Jamie has spent a good portion of the trip contorted into shapes trying to reach parts of the boat not primarily designed to facilitate human access.

"The most used item in our mountain of Amazon pre-trip deliveries was a cheap endoscope; a tiny camera on a long and stiff but bendable cable that connects directly into an iPhone. Absolutely invaluable for looking into all those places otherwise impossible to see or reach, before resorting to taking it all apart."

He certainly knows a lot more about the systems on an offshore cruising yacht now than when we started!

Jamie grew up sailing and has been passionate about all things water-based ever since. The opportunity to live this adventure and to share it with the family is a dream come true.

Sportsman or hunter-gatherer? The boundary feels blurred at times! Eating fresh fish as much as we do is a real privilege, it's just fortunate that the process is as fun and rewarding as the result.

A tell-tale sign of how easy a passage is: how much fishing we're doing. On the rough ones, not so much!

- section_label: In the water image_side: left image: /assets/images/crew/jamie/S-I-A-drifting.webp image_alt: Drifting through a coral atoll pass eyebrow: Drifting through a coral atoll pass heading: The real magic is in the water text: |

The real magic (in the tropics at least) is under the surface. We have been fortunate to swim, snorkel and dive in some of the worlds most remote coral atolls and clearest waters.

Drifting through a coral pass, surrounded by schools of fish and sharks, never loses its appeal or novelty. Trying to balance recording our experiences with just taking them in, we hope to return with a full memory bank we can never forget.
