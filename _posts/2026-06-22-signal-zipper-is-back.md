Today has been a great day.

We are moored off Ilot Signal — a small reef island inside New Caledonia's main lagoon — in crystal-clear water, snorkelling with reef fish and turtles, and we finally feel that we are back living the dream this trip was always about. Much as we've loved our time in New Zealand ([health scares apart](https://jnbreid79.github.io/reids-unzipped/2025/11/28/a-year-in-whangarei.html)), it's been a long time — almost 20 months — since we left Tonga for New Zealand back in October 2024.

![Zipper moored in the lagoon off Ilot Signal]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gallery/2026-06/ilot-signal-anchorage.webp)

## A Week in Nouméa

We had a great week in Nouméa catching up with lots of friends also arriving from New Zealand. Some of them had considerably more dramatic passages than we did — skillfully and calmly overcoming terrifying breakages in rough conditions. We are genuinely grateful for our uneventful crossing, even if it felt a bit on the edge to us at times.

There is plenty to enjoy in Nouméa. The fruit, veg and fish markets are excellent, and after so long in New Zealand the French influence — boulangeries, and finally some proper cheese — was very welcome. Why, with all the dairy farms in New Zealand, is there so little proper cheese? Not so much a gap in the market as a chasm, surely someone could fill that!

We had a wonderful day trip north to Fort Teremba with Bumpy before he left us after the passage — the history of the penal colony and the surrounding countryside were well worth the trip. Closer to town we enjoyed great afternoons at both the maritime museum and the aquarium. We stayed longer than we'd otherwise have planned because of the music festival on Saturday; free music in venues throughout town, all afternoon and evening, with several kid boats joining up for the occasion. Enjoyed by all ages.

## Ilot Signal

![The beach at Ilot Signal]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gallery/2026-06/ilot-signal-beach.webp)
![New Caledonia's lagoon from the island path]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gallery/2026-06/new-caledonia-lagoon.webp)

Ilot Signal is a nature reserve, with a path roughly a mile long around the island's perimeter. Birdlife everywhere, and superb views — the New Caledonia mountains rising to the east, waves breaking on the outer reef to the west. We were puzzled at first by the large burrows along the edges of the path, slightly alarmed at the thought of how big the crabs or snakes must be — until we discovered that many of the petrels and shearwaters actually dig burrows to nest in. Phew!

The highlight, though, was an enormous osprey nest - a huge structure up in a barren tree; not exactly hidden away. Presumably that means there's nothing here that an osprey needs to hide from. We could see a fledgling peering over the edge of the nest, and later spotted what must have been a parent in a nearby tree keeping a lookout.

## In the Water

The white sand was beautifully soft, and the water — though not quite bath temperature (we need to head further north for that) — was substantially warmer than than it had felt recently in New Zealand. Snorkelling off the main reef, even briefly, a turtle swam right past us and we saw numerous striking trigger fish, clown fish in their anenomies and were even surprised by a lion fish. No sea kraits though, perhaps fortunately as we gather they are extremely poisonous!

![The reef at Ilot Signal]({{ site.baseurl }}/assets/images/gallery/2026-06/ilot-signal-reef.webp)


## Back at anchor in the tropics, finally

To top it all off we had friends with families from a couple of other boats onboard for a drink in the evening. So many people have done so much in the last year and a half to make this day possible, we are endlessly grateful, and determined to make the most of the privileged opportunity we now have.
