Guest appearances

Friends, family & hitchhikers who’ve joined along the way

On our way round we are welcoming family and friends aboard at various times — both to come and say hi, and to help with the sailing, particularly on longer passages.

B
Bumpy (Nigel) Shakedown: Cherbourg → St Malo

Bumpy joined us for our very first passage on Zipper, helping us learn how to sail Zipper for the first time. We anchored off Sark overnight on the way.

T
Tom Bay of Biscay: Roscoff → Lanzarote

We needed someone experienced onboard! Tom was MVP on this passage, for the Hill Climb game on his phone — months of playtime prepared!

N
Neal Bay of Biscay: Roscoff → Lanzarote

Building experience for Wind Rider and a planned circumnavigation. Closely fought for MVP with Tom, but second place with Wordscape. Another winner on the phone games though.

J
Dottie (Julia) Atlantic crossing: Tenerife → Mindelo → Barbados

Treasure hunt director and morale booster, keeping the children on their toes every other day for prizes hidden around the boat and arranging milestone celebrations.

R
Grumpy (Rob) Atlantic crossing: Tenerife → Mindelo → Barbados

Daily sunset optimist (Rob "the clouds are definitely going to light up this evening" Mackay), emergency fresh water taster and welcome icecream supplier.

P
Paul Panama canal: Shelter Bay → Panama City

Star long-distance line handler flying in from Massachusetts, ticking one more adventure off his bucket list .

N
Bumpy (Nigel, aka Neptune) Pacific crossing: Panama (inc. canal) → Galapagos → Marquesas

Stand-in King Neptune, team photographer, music composer ("The Good Ship Zipper") and remarkably energy-efficient, space-saving automatic dishwasher.

P
Paul (the return) South Pacific: Tonga → New Zealand

Answering an SOS for additional crew having missed our planned passage with Hamish due to inclement weather, Paul 'popped over' at short notice. Not sure this ugly passage was on his bucket list, but Paul was the perfect addition to the crew nevertheless!

M
Marc Hitchhiker: 29°22'S 174°14'E (near John's corner) → New Zealand

Co-skipper of the good ship Agápē, may she rest in peace. We met Marc, Leticia and Deane in what became Agápē's final hours. After sharing some airtime in rough seas and near-gale conditions approaching NZ, trying to reef the jib on the trampolines, Jamie and Marc did some serious bonding!

L
Leticia Hitchhiker: John's corner to New Zealand

Co-skipper of Agápē, in Leticia we have made another great friend. We are just delighted we were able to bring the crew home safely.

D
Deane Hitchhiker: John's corner to New Zealand

Crew for his great buddies on Agápē, Deane was testing his own appetite for the long distance passages. Not sure anyone anticipated his experience would involve a few days on a 45ft catamaran, but he provided great company through the night watches and we were delighted to have him onboard!
