My name is Isla Reid and I am 10 years old. My family is British — there's me, my brother Angus, my mum Sophie, and my dad Jamie. We've been living on our catamaran Zipper and sailing around the world together.

I love swimming, dancing, singing, writing, reading and drawing.

We are very lucky. I've been to places most people never get to see, and I've met people and sea creatures I would never have come across if we'd stayed at home in England.

# ── Feature rows ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── features: - section_label: Life on the water image_side: left image: /assets/images/crew/isla-tumbling.webp image_alt: Isla diving on the coral in the Tuamotus eyebrow: The sea heading: "Crystal clear in Bonaire and the Tuamotus — pretty amazing everywhere else too" text: |

The water has been one of the best things about the whole trip. In Bonaire and the Tuamotus it was crystal clear — you could see everything right down to the bottom, all the coral and the fish just going about their day. But honestly, it was great in almost every place we've been.

In the Tuamotus we drift-snorkelled out through the passes on the current — you just float and the ocean carries you, and there are sharks and rays and thousands of fish all around you.

Swimming is one of my favourite things, so being on a boat where you can jump in basically whenever you want has been pretty brilliant.

- section_label: The journey image_side: right image: /assets/images/crew/isla/isla-free.webp image_alt: Isla taking in the fresh air in the Marquesas, French Polynesia eyebrow: Visiting difference places heading: "From France to New Zealand — and everywhere between" text: |

We started in France and have sailed to the Canary Islands, the Cape Verde islands, the Caribbean, Bonaire, Panama, the Galápagos, French Polynesia — including the Marquesas and the Tuamotus — then the Cook Islands, Tonga, and finally New Zealand.

Going to school in Whangarei was so different from home — 500 pupils instead of 80.
