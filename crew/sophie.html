Sophie's main job isn't actually very different to that on land — keep everyone healthy, safe and in one piece, whilst helping guide them along the way. The guidance is a little more formal these days (but not much) and to be honest, there are times when it's questionable who is learning more; Sophie or the children!

And keeping everyone fit and healthy can be more of a challenge at times — who wants to eat and drink when it feels like you are being tumbled around in a washing machine, and it's hard enough standing up when the waves are coming at you from all angles — surely that's enough exercise.

# ── Feature rows ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── features: - section_label: Life at anchor image_side: left image: /assets/images/crew/sophie/sophie-dinghy.webp image_alt: Sophie heading ashore to explore with the family eyebrow: New horizons heading: "A new rhythm of exploration and discovery" text: |

But then we get somewhere new, put the anchor down and life settles into a new rhythm of exploration and discovery. A new culture to immerse ourselves in; new foods to experiment with; and a new playground in and under the sea; and always new friends to be made.

It feels like life takes care of itself sometimes (if you ignore all the boatwork etc. that is always part of this lifestyle). We are all learning, all the time and it is incredible.

- section_label: The medical department image_side: right image: /assets/images/crew/sophie/sophie-campfire.webp image_alt: Sophie keeping the crew going with a campfire eyebrow: 4 whole days of training heading: "Savlon and a plaster — you'll be fine" text: |

Luckily Sophie's extensive medical training (4 whole days!) has not yet been called upon for more than the occasional scrape and is generally covered in the 'put savlon and a plaster on it, you'll be fine' category. She's hoping her skills are not going to be tested too much as they may not match up to her father-in-law and brother-in-law, both doctors!
